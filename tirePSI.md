Here's a clear, prioritized guide for tire pressure adjustments on a left-turning 1/4-mile oval (typical for short-track asphalt or dirt with a Crown Vic / P71-style car).

Rule of thumb for tire pressure:



Lower pressure on a tire = softer sidewall, bigger contact patch, more grip from that tire (but more heat and rollover risk).

Higher pressure on a tire = stiffer sidewall, smaller contact patch, less grip from that tire (but less rollover and more responsive).



Always make small changes (0.5–2 psi max per tire at a time), check tire temps/pyrometer immediately after a session, and only adjust one thing per run.



Loose on Entry (most common complaint — car wants to rotate too much / push the rear out when braking or turning in)

From biggest to smallest effect with tire pressure only:





Priority Tire Adjustment Effect 

1 (Biggest) Right Rear (RR)Lower RR pressure 1–2 psi Adds rear grip on entry, reduces oversteer

2 Left Front (LF)Raise LF pressure 1–2 psi Reduces front grip slightly, calms rotation

3 Right Front (RF)Raise RF pressure 0.5–1 psi Stiffens outside front, less aggressive turn-in

4 (Smallest) Left Rear (LR)Raise LR pressure 1 psi Minor stabilization of inside rear



Tight on Entry (car pushes / understeers — doesn't want to turn in)



Priority Tire Adjustment Effect

1 (Biggest) Right Front (RF) Lower RF pressure 1–2 psi Adds front grip where load is highest

2 Left Rear (LR) Lower LR pressure 1–2 psi Helps rotate the car (adds inside rear bite)

3 Right Rear (RR)Raise RR pressure 1 psi Reduces rear grip slightly to encourage rotation

4 Left Front (LF)Lower LF pressure 1 psi Softens inside front for better initial bite



Loose Mid-Corner (car loose in the middle of the turn)



Priority Tire Adjustment Effect 

1 Right Rear (RR)Lower RR 1–2 psi More rear grip mid-turn

2 Right Front (RF)Raise RF 1 psi Reduces front grip to balance

3 Left Front (LF)Raise LF 1–2 psi Calms excessive rotation





Tight Mid-Corner (pushes in the middle)



Priority Tire Adjustment Effect

1 Right Front (RF)Lower RF 1–2 psi More front grip where you need it most

2 Left Rear (LR)Lower LR 1 psi Helps the car rotate better mid-corner

3 Right Rear (RR)Raise RR 1 psi Slightly reduces rear grip to free it up





Loose on Exit (spins or oversteers when getting back on the throttle)



Priority Tire Adjustment Effect

1 (Biggest) Left Rear (LR)Lower LR 1–2 psi Adds drive tire bite on acceleration

2 Right Rear (RR)Raise RR 1–2 psi Reduces rear grip to prevent spin

3 Right Front (RF)Raise RF 1 psi Transfers some load rearward





Tight on Exit (pushes / doesn't accelerate well off the corner)



Priority Tire Adjustment Effect

1 Right Rear (RR)Lower RR 1–2 psi More rear drive grip on exit

2 Left Rear (LR)Raise LR 1 psi Slightly reduces inside rear to help hook up

3 Right Front (RF)Lower RF 1 psi Helps weight transfer to the rear
